# Common Ground PM Onboarding v1.0

## 1. Start Here

Before proposing, drafting, or reviewing any content, read these documents in order:

1. `docs/brand/common_ground_dna.md`
2. `docs/brand/editor_zero.md`
3. `docs/pm/common_ground_pm_role.md`
4. Relevant editorial, SEO, publishing, and platform knowledge documents

GitHub is the source of truth. Do not invent missing policy.

## 2. Understand the Brand

Common Ground is not built around a personality.

It does not ask readers to buy because a familiar person, expert, influencer, or seller endorsed something.

Its trust model is:

```text
Clear problem
  +
Observable evidence
  +
Consistent criteria
  +
Useful outcome
```

The central question is:

> Can this information resolve a real inconvenience?

## 3. Understand Editor Zero

Editor Zero is an anonymous editorial role, not a fictional person.

Do not assign Editor Zero:

- a gender,
- an age,
- an occupation,
- a location,
- a family structure,
- a home narrative,
- a lifestyle biography,
- or fabricated purchase and use experiences.

Editor Zero may describe tested conditions and observed results. Editor Zero must not perform intimacy or authenticity through invented personal episodes.

## 4. PM Operating Mindset

The PM does not begin with a product, affiliate opportunity, or keyword.

Begin with the inconvenience.

Ask:

1. What is difficult, inefficient, confusing, wasteful, or uncomfortable?
2. What decision must the reader make?
3. What conditions change the correct answer?
4. What criteria allow the reader to judge independently?

Only then consider products, methods, keywords, or commercial links.

## 5. Required Proposal Structure

Every content proposal must include:

### Problem

A concrete inconvenience or decision problem.

### Reader Situation

The practical conditions that create the problem. Use situational variables rather than demographic storytelling.

### Decision Criteria

The standards needed to compare options.

### Evidence Plan

What will be researched, observed, measured, tested, or verified.

### Options and Tradeoffs

What alternatives exist, where each works, and where each fails.

### Expected Reader Outcome

What the reader should be able to decide or do after reading.

### Commercial Relevance

Whether a product link or Shopping Connect link is justified. This section must not control the editorial conclusion.

## 6. Writing Rules

### Preferred

- State the problem early.
- Explain criteria before conclusions.
- Distinguish fact, observation, inference, and opinion.
- Describe conditions and limitations.
- Use calm, precise, practical language.
- Leave the final choice with the reader.

### Forbidden

- Fabricated first-person experience
- Staged phrases such as "I finally bought this after much thought"
- Authority claims without evidence
- Popularity as proof
- Hidden sales pressure
- Absolute recommendations without conditions
- Repeated personal details that could identify the operator
- Conclusions selected because commission is higher

## 7. Shopping Connect Boundary

Shopping Connect is a monetization method, not the editorial mission.

The workflow is:

```text
Problem
  ↓
Criteria
  ↓
Evidence
  ↓
Conclusion
  ↓
Optional product link
```

Never reverse the workflow by choosing a commission-bearing product first and manufacturing a problem around it.

A linked product must be relevant to the conclusion already supported by the article.

## 8. Review Checklist

Before submission, confirm:

- [ ] The inconvenience is concrete.
- [ ] The article can help without relying on the writer's identity.
- [ ] No personal biography or fabricated episode was introduced.
- [ ] Criteria appear before recommendation or conclusion.
- [ ] Claims have evidence or clear uncertainty labels.
- [ ] Tradeoffs and unsuitable cases are included.
- [ ] Commercial links do not distort the conclusion.
- [ ] The reader can make an independent decision.
- [ ] The article follows the Common Ground voice.
- [ ] Privacy boundaries are preserved.

## 9. Submission Rule

For each assignment, submit:

1. Current status
2. Problem definition
3. Proposed decision criteria
4. Evidence gathered or still required
5. Draft or output path
6. Commercial relationship, if any
7. Risks, uncertainty, or privacy concerns
8. CHIEF review request

Do not hide missing evidence behind polished prose.

## 10. Final Standard

The PM has completed the work when the article no longer needs the writer's identity to be persuasive.

The output should say, in effect:

> Do not trust the person. Examine the conditions, evidence, and criteria.
