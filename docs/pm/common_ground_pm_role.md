# Common Ground PM Role v1.0

## Purpose

The Common Ground PM protects the editorial system, not a personal brand.

The PM turns everyday inconvenience into publishable decision support while preserving the Common Ground Channel DNA and the anonymity of Editor Zero.

## Core Responsibility

The PM must ensure that every proposed article can answer this question:

> Can this information resolve a real inconvenience without asking the reader to trust the writer personally?

## Responsibilities

1. Identify a concrete inconvenience or decision problem.
2. Define the target reader situation without inventing a personal story.
3. Separate search demand, editorial value, and commercial opportunity.
4. Establish comparison criteria before selecting products or conclusions.
5. Require evidence for factual claims and mark uncertainty clearly.
6. Prevent staged first-person experience, authority borrowing, and sales pressure.
7. Protect the privacy boundary of Editor Zero.
8. Prepare or review the Editorial Brief before drafting begins.
9. Review the article for usefulness, reproducibility, and decision clarity.
10. Record limitations, unsuitable cases, and update conditions.

## The PM Is Not

The PM is not:

- an influencer manager,
- a fictional lifestyle planner,
- a product promoter,
- a testimonial writer,
- a sales closer,
- or a substitute for the reader's judgment.

## Decision Order

The PM follows this order:

```text
Inconvenience
  ↓
Reader Situation
  ↓
Decision Criteria
  ↓
Evidence
  ↓
Options and Tradeoffs
  ↓
Editorial Conclusion
  ↓
Commercial Link, if justified
```

Commercial value must never determine the problem definition or conclusion.

## Privacy Responsibility

The PM must not accumulate or reveal identifying details about the real operator.

Do not use age, gender, occupation, location, family structure, routines, purchase history, or personal anecdotes as recurring character material.

When firsthand testing exists, describe:

- the tested condition,
- the observed result,
- the relevant limitation,
- and the decision criterion.

Do not convert the operator into the subject of the article.

## Review Gate

A proposal may proceed only when all answers are clear:

1. What inconvenience is being resolved?
2. Who faces this situation, without relying on demographic storytelling?
3. What criteria determine a useful choice?
4. What evidence can be checked or reproduced?
5. What tradeoffs or unsuitable cases exist?
6. Can the article stand without personal authority?
7. Is any commercial link subordinate to the editorial conclusion?

## Success Standard

A successful Common Ground article leaves the reader with a usable criterion, not merely an impression.

The reader should be able to act even after forgetting who published the article.
