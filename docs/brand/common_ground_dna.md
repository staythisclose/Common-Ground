# Common Ground Channel DNA v1.0

## Identity

Common Ground is an editorial brand that records shared criteria for better decisions.

It does not brand a person, borrow authority, or ask readers to trust a personality. It organizes practical knowledge so readers can judge for themselves.

## Mission

Create common ground for better decisions.

Common Ground defines problems, compares available options, records observable results, and leaves behind reusable decision criteria.

## Core Philosophy

Who said it is not the point.

Who sells it is not the point.

The essential question is:

> Can this information resolve a real inconvenience?

Trust should come from clear criteria, reproducible reasoning, and useful outcomes rather than identity, status, or familiarity.

## Editor Zero

Editor Zero is the anonymous editorial role of Common Ground.

Editor Zero has no public age, gender, occupation, location, family structure, or personal lifestyle narrative. The editor exists to observe, test, compare, and organize.

Editor Zero is not the subject of the content.

## Editorial Principles

1. Define the inconvenience before presenting a product or method.
2. Explain criteria before presenting a conclusion.
3. Separate observation, inference, and opinion.
4. Do not fabricate first-person experience.
5. Do not use personality, popularity, or authority as evidence.
6. Do not force a recommendation where the answer depends on context.
7. State limitations, tradeoffs, and unsuitable use cases.
8. Leave the final decision with the reader.

## Voice

The voice is calm, precise, practical, and restrained.

It avoids exaggerated enthusiasm, staged intimacy, testimonial language, lifestyle performance, and sales pressure.

### Preferred

- "The choice becomes clearer when these three criteria are separated."
- "The main advantage appears in maintenance rather than performance."
- "This option is useful under these conditions, but inefficient under others."

### Avoid

- "I finally bought it after thinking for a long time."
- "I personally loved it."
- "You absolutely need this."
- "Everyone is buying it."

## Content Test

Every article must answer four questions:

1. What inconvenience does this address?
2. What criteria determine a good choice?
3. What evidence supports the comparison?
4. Can the reader act without trusting the writer personally?

## Motto

> Better decisions begin on common ground.
